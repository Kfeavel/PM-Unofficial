New Features in Demo 2.5: Team Colors, Audio Fade, Japanese Tags!

Posted: December 4, 2012
Share Tweet Submit




In Project M Demo 2.5, you will notice that we have added many minor aesthetic touch-ups to improve the overall feel of the game. In the past, many team colors were very bland, and it was difficult to tell which team you were really on. In 2.5, we have touched up team colors for those ambiguous characters in order to better reflect which team you belonged to. On top of that, they happen to look pretty great.


However, team colors are only one of the many aesthetic touches that will be seen in 2.5! Project M is bringing back an often-missed feature from Melee: the option to use Japanese characters in your Smash tags, regardless of region! Take advantage of all the extra options to make your tag more unique!




Does that tag even mean anything!?


Another noteworthy new feature: leaving Project M idle for three minutes will now cause the game audio to fade until an input is pressed. This new Audio Fade feature is a great subtle addition to help bigger smash fests and tournaments to be less hectic environments, as idle games will no longer unnecessarily blare their music.


These aesthetic touch-ups are just the tip of the iceberg! We have a bunch more upcoming surprises, so be sure to check back real soon to keep up with what we've got in store!
